“In an era in which precision medicine and personalized healthcare dominate discussion, the pharmaceutical industry must prioritize the voices of those it serves: patients,” write Allison Martin, regulation and policy lead at the drug company Sanofi, and Russ Paulsen, the head of the nonprofit UsAgainstAlzheimer’s. The approach should involve including data on quality of life in the drug development process , collected through surveys and interviews, “to bridge gaps between clinical endpoints and patient realities”. This approach is particularly crucial for neurodegenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer’s, where symptoms have profound emotional impacts on those afflicted and their caregivers, they note.
